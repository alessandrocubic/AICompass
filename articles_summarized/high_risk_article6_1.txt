The obligations under the Union harmonization legislation require manufacturers and providers across various industries, such as machinery, toys, medical devices, and protective equipment, to comply with safety, performance, and regulatory standards. These include ensuring products meet specific safety requirements, following standardized procedures for equipment in hazardous environments, and ensuring conformity with EU regulations before placing products on the market. The legislation aims to harmonize laws across EU member states, ensuring consistent safety and regulatory compliance in areas like lifts, radio equipment, medical devices, and personal protective equipment.
